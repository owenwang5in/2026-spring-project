Methods😶
Study Design

We started our study by watching the documentary Twelve Nights to learn about animal shelters and abandoned animals. Then, we gathered information from professionals and visited a shelter. After that, we used the fishbone diagram and “5 Whys” method to find our research topic and create our research question with help from our instructor.

1) Initial observation of situation/subject
- Watch the documentary of Twelve Nights
2) Getting information so that you can design the survey accurately
1. Getting information from professional people
2. Going to the shelter
3) Find research topic
1. Fish bone
2. 5 whys
3. Find action point
4) Come up with research question
1. Do online research
2. Putting all the questions together
3. Discussing questions with instructor
4. Revise the question with instructor advice


Participants

The participants were people who completed our survey. We collected at least 50 responses, including a maximum of 10 Daan students and 5 Daan teachers.

- People that participated in the survey
- 50 people minimum
- 10 Daan students maximum
- 5 Daan teachers maximum


Materials/Tools

We used survey question lists, a microphone, a camera, stationery, and posters with questions and answer choices. Our group also used additional materials when needed.

- Survey (design for collecting data)
- Question list
- Microphone
- Camera
- Stationery
- Poster for questions and choices


Procedure

First, we formed groups and assigned jobs such as speaker, recorder, and helper. Then, we went to the survey location, introduced our project, asked questions face-to-face, and recorded the answers.

1) Form groups
2) Assign jobs for survey
- Speaker: Catch people and tell them what we are doing. Tell them the question
- Recorder: Write down the answer
- Helper: Hold the things to show the options of the questions
3) Go to place for survey
4) Conducting the survey
1. Introduce ourselves and our project
2. Collecting data with face-to-face means
3. Ask the question before asking their age
4. Ask if they want results


Special Considerations

We spoke clearly, stayed respectful, and tried to be consistent during the survey. We also avoided bias and did not pressure people to participate.

- Giving the survey
- Speak loudly
- Find a good place
- don’t ask more than one person at the same time
- consistent
- don’t change the introduction
- don’t switch job
- be respectful
- don’t say bad word
- don’t sit down
- don’t chase the people
- avoid bias


Data Analysis

We organized the survey answers by compiling and sorting the data. Then, we created visualizations, analyzed patterns, and made conclusions based on the results.

1) Processing the data: (Steps that organize the data so it is easy to read and see patterns)
1. Compile the answers
2. Sort the data
3. Visualize data

2) Analyzing the data: (Explanation of important things we see when we look at the data)
1. Describe the data (results are region specific)
2. Conclude the data



Questionnaires❓

To gather quantitative data, our team chiefly relied on questionnaires and detailed observation checklists. During the actual field research, we used cameras and microphones to record the entire process. Additionally, we set up computers to present choices clearly, ensuring that all participants could easily understand and accurately complete the survey.
