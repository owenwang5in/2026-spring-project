Survey Question Results
Question 1: Have you ever owned a dog?

The survey question was "Have you ever owned a dog?" The goal was to learn about respondents’ experience with dog ownership. This was a single-choice question that collected categorical data. A bar graph was used because it makes the categories easy to compare. The most popular response was "Never owned a dog" (36.67%), while the least popular response was 31.67%. A noticeable pattern was that the percentages of people who currently own a dog and those who previously owned a dog were the same.


Question 2: How much do you think the average annual medical expenses per year for owning a dog would be?

The survey question was "How much do you think the average annual medical expenses for owning a dog would be?" The goal was to understand people’s estimates of yearly dog medical costs. This was an open-ended question that collected numerical data. Responses were grouped into value ranges for analysis. A line graph was used to show the overall pattern of responses. The most common estimate was 16.7%, while the least common was 1.7%. Many respondents estimated higher costs than expected.


Question 3: What do you think is the main reason preventing people from adopting a dog?

The survey question was "What do you think is the main reason preventing people from adopting a dog?" The goal was to identify factors that discourage dog adoption. This was a multiple-selection question that collected categorical data. A bar graph was used because it makes it easy to compare the frequency of each reason. The most common response was selected by 50% of respondents, while the least common response was selected by 3%. The results showed that some reasons were much more common than others.


Question 4: How would you respond if your dog has unusual behavior (such as loss of appetite or vomiting)?

The survey question was "What will you do if your dog has unusual behavior (such as loss of appetite or vomiting)?" The goal was to understand how people would respond to signs of illness in their dog. This was a single-choice question that collected categorical data. A bar graph was used because it clearly compares the frequency of each response. The most common answer was selected by 48.33% of respondents, while the least common was 10%. The results supported our prediction that most people would take their dog to a veterinarian immediately when unusual behavior occurs.


Question 5: If the cost of medical care for dogs decreases in the future, how will this affect your willingness to adopt a dog?

The survey question was “If the cost of medical care for dogs decreases in the future, how will this affect your willingness to adopt a dog?” The goal was to see whether lower costs would affect adoption willingness. This was a single-choice question using a 1–5 scale (numerical data). A bar graph was used to compare how many people chose each rating. The most common response was 40%, while the least common was 1.67%. The results showed that lower medical costs did not strongly change many people’s willingness to adopt, suggesting other factors are also important.
