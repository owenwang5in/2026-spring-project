Story

Minnie was just a normal dog in her own world. One day, while she was running through a forest, she saw a strange glowing portal. Being curious, she walked closer and accidentally fell through it. When she opened her eyes, she was on Earth. Everything looked different. There were huge buildings, loud noises, and strange creatures walking on two legs. Minnie had never seen humans before. She watched them from a distance, confused and scared. Since she did not know what humans were, she thought they were monsters. She wanted to go home, but the portal had disappeared completely forever.

As Minnie wandered around, things did not get any easier. Some people yelled at her to go away. Others ignored her when she searched for food. A few even scared her on purpose. Minnie became more frightened every day. Since she believed humans were monsters, their actions only made her think more about it . She spent her nights hiding behind buildings and sleeping on cold sidewalks. She missed her home and her family. The city felt rhuge and dangerous. Every time she saw a human walking toward her, she would run away as fast as her.little legs could carry her.

After a few days, Minnie was very hungry and thirsty. She could barely find anything to eat, and she felt weaker each day. Her fur became dirty, and she no longer had the energy to run far. One afternoon, she collapsed near a park. Minnie thought she would never see her home again. She closed her eyes and waited. Then she heard a gentle voice. A woman was kneeling beside her with a bowl of water. Minnie was scared at first, but she was so thirsty that she slowly drank from the bowl without moving away.

The woman carefully took Minnie to an animal shelter. Minnie expected another scary place, but it was warm and safe. The workers gave her food, water, and a soft bed to sleep on. Nobody shouted at her or chased her away. For the first time since arriving on Earth, Minnie felt safe. She slowly realized that not all humans were monsters. Some were actually kind. Over the next few weeks, she got stronger and made new friends. Even though she still missed her world, Minnie was happy because she finally had people who cared about her.
