Methods

methodology

Study Design

The project has 4 stages. First, we watched Twelve Nights to understand animal shelter issues. Next, we collected information by interviewing experts and visiting shelters. Then, they used methods such as the Fishbone diagram and “5 Whys” to identify the main cause of the problem and focus on one action. At last , we developed our research questions.

Participants

The respondents in this survey were the people from the general public, and a. The sample size was expected to be at least 50 people. To avoid the sample being too concentrated within the school, this study specifically set sample limits: a maximum of 2 student respondents from Da'an Junior High School, to ensure that the data has a certain degree of social representativeness.

Materials/Tools

The main research tools were questionnaires and data collection forms. During the field survey, the research team used microphones and cameras to record the process and prepared computers to show answer choices so respondents could understand the questions more easily.

Procedure

The study was carried out in two Parts: team role assignment and on-site survey procedures. Team members were given three roles: the speaker introduced the study and asked questions, the recorder documented responses, and the helper displayed answer options on a laptop to assist respondents. During the survey, researchers introduced themselves and explained the purpose of the study to gain participants’ consent. Interviews followed a standardized process, focusing first on key questions such as stray dog shelter and medical expenses, followed by basic demographic information like age. At the end of each interview, respondents were asked if they would like to receive the final survey results.

Special Considerations

To make sure the survey was reliable and professional, the team followed several guidelines. Researchers needed to choose appropriate locations and speak clearly and confidently. Every participant received the same introduction script to keep the survey consistent, and team members stayed in their assigned roles. Researchers were also expected to act respectfully and professionally by avoiding inappropriate language, standing while interviewing, and not following or pressuring people to participate. In addition, interviews were conducted individually

Data Analysis

The analysis consists of two parts : data processing and interpretation. First,we collected and organized, categorized, and presented to identify key trends and patterns. Next, the results are interpreted to explain region-specific results and determine how financial perceptions influence adoption behavior overall.
