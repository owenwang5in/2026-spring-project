Introduction

Abstract

Following the implementation of the "no-kill" policy in Taiwan, public animal shelters have faced a significant increase in dog numbers. Despite the growing number of dogs awaiting homes, adoption rates are still low, creating a need to understand the barriers to pet adoption. This study investigates the factors causing people to hesitate when considering dog adoption, with a specific focus on how expected veterinary medical costs influence their final decisions.This study implements a four-stage research design, combining field observations with a convenience sampling questionnaire survey of the general public (N = 60). Data were collected through standardized on-site interview protocols and analyzed using descriptive statistics, data visualization, and Chi-square tests to examine behavioral relationships. We found that financial burden and limited living space were the main barriers to dog adoption.
