What is Good?

Some people think that making jokes however they want is fine, as they are only joking. But even if one had no intention of hurting another, making jokes that might offend someone still makes it a bad thing to do. If one were to make jokes, the jokes they make should be non-offensive to be good ones, and to be a good person.

Making good jokes makes everyone comfortable. Harmless and silly jokes can lift the spirits of others, hence the common saying that “laughter is the best medicine”, and can also lead to better growth of the community. Good jokes also spread good vibes, which creates a positive atmosphere, allowing everyone to feel welcomed and appreciated.

Meanwhile with bad jokes, many problems can arise from it. The overuse of offensive jokes could cause offensive jokes to be normalized, causing toxic relationships of constantly weaponizing jokes to form. People might also feel attacked by or feel insecure because of your jokes, which can cause people to dislike or even hate you.

Good jokes and bad jokes can both affect people’s health, effectively making them two sides of the same coin. Some jokes might pry at others’ insecurities, which could cause their mental health to deteriorate, while on the other hand, good jokes can keep one’s wellbeing healthy.

In conclusion, whatever we say, even if we think that it won’t have any impact on others, can still greatly affect others. We should all watch what we’re saying, even when trying to make one harmless joke, as it’s better to be safe than sorry.
