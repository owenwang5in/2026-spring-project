Analysis

Data Analysis

According to our survey results, we can clearly see that most people do not remember animal shelter advertisements very well. Out of 80 respondents, 70% said they do not remember seeing an animal shelter advertisement in the past two years. Among those who did remember seeing one, many could not clearly describe the content. In fact, 76.2% of respondents said they could not explain the advertisement clearly, while only 23.8% said they could.

The results suggest that animal shelter advertisements may not be memorable enough to leave a strong impression on viewers. If people cannot remember the advertisement or its message, it may be difficult for the advertisement to encourage animal adoption or raise awareness about shelter animals.

For the question about what information should be emphasized in animal shelter advertisements, Option A, the iInformation about the dogs was chosen by 85% of respondents, making it the most popular choice. Options B, the location of shelter (68.8%) and D contact information (60%) were also selected by many participants. In contrast, Options E music (5%) and F connection to current popular topics (3.8%) were chosen by very few people. This shows that respondents clearly prefer certain types of information and believe some messages are much more important than others.

Most of the respondents were teenagers aged 13–17 years old (57.5%), and 70% of all respondents were female. Because of this, the survey results mainly reflect the opinions of younger people and may not fully represent all age groups.

Conclusion

The survey results show people have different opinions about shelter advertisements and dog adoption, then for now animal shelter advertisements may not be effective, because many respondents said they could not clearly remember the advertisements.

This may mean the shelter can improve the stronger story, emotional content, and clearer messages about adoption. It also shows that the advertisements may not be good enough at sharing their message with people. So, in our opinions, the result is the combination of what we thought.

In the future, we want to know “Why will women care more about dogs?”, “If they want to adopt the dog, what kind of benefit do they want? ”, “Why do people still think spokesperson or representative are important?”, and we want to ask more responses from different age groups, more male, to make sure the surveys are more complete and representative.

The results of this survey can help animal shelters plan better advertisements in the future. It may help them make more effective advertisements and make more people care about and adopt shelter animals.
