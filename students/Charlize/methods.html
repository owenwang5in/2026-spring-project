Materials/Tools
In our survey, we have four questions about advertisements from animal shelters. This survey aims to find out how effective animal shelter advertisements are and how they can be improved. We want to learn how many people remember seeing shelter ads, how many are influenced to adopt pets, and what kinds of advertisements are easiest for the public to remember. The survey also looks at which parts of an advertisement attract the most attention, such as the design, information, music, or popular topics. By collecting this information, we can understand whether people think the advertisements are effective and give advice to shelters on how to create better and more memorable ads.
- Microphone
- Camera
- Stationery
- Poster for questions and choices
- Costume (dog costume)

Procedure
First, we formed groups and assigned different jobs for the survey. One person introduced the project and asked the questions, another recorded the answers, and another helped by showing the question options. Then, we went to the survey location and conducted face-to-face surveys by introducing ourselves, collecting data, and asking questions before asking the participants’ ages.
1 Form groups
2 Assign jobs for survey
- Speaker: Catch people and tell them what we are doing. Tell them the question
- Recorder: Write down the answer
- Helper: Hold the things to show the options of the questions
3 Go to place for survey
4 Conducting the survey
1. Introduce ourselves and our project
2. Collecting data with face-to-face means
3. Ask the question before asking their age

Special Considerations
When giving the survey, we made sure to speak loudly and clearly while only asking one person at a time. We stayed consistent by keeping the same introduction and not switching jobs. We also acted respectfully by avoiding bad words, not sitting down, and not chasing people. Lastly, we tried to avoid bias so the survey results would be fair and accurate.
- Giving the survey
- Speak loudly
- don’t ask more than one person at the same time
