People who have mental health problems are not always bad. They are born with the illness and they
cannot control themselves. They might have already tried their best and taken those pills to keep themselves
in control but it just is not effective.


Think about it, there is a girl who always smiles and helps her classmates when they are sad. She
listens to their problems and tries her best to make them feel better. But she has a mental health problem
that she was born with, and sometimes she suddenly feels extremely angry or very sad without knowing why.
She tries to control herself and even takes medicine every day, but it does not always work. One day,
she accidentally shouts at her friend and pushes her away during an emotional moment. Other people see this
and think she is a bad person, but they don’t know how hard she has been trying to stay calm.
She feels guilty and cries alone, wishing she could be like everyone else. Is she really a bad person,
or is she someone who is struggling but still trying her best?

What if there is a homeless man who sleeps on the streets and often asks people for food. Many people
walk past him and think he cannot do anything useful because he needs help from others. But one day, he sees
a car accident happen right in front of him.
Without thinking, he runs over to help the injured people, calls for help, and stays with them until the
ambulance arrives. Even though he does not have a home and needs help in his own life, he still chooses to
help others when they are in danger. He might not have money or a place to live, but he has courage and
kindness.
Is he someone useless, or someone who can still make a difference?
