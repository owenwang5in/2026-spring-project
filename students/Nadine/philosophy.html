What is good?

What does it mean to be good? Many people think being good is simply about following rules or being nice. However, I believe being good means having the courage to do what is right, following our faith and values even when others disagree, and listening to our conscience instead of giving in to peer pressure. First, doing what is right takes courage, especially when others disagree with us. Sometimes, we choose to do the right thing even though many people do not support us. We might feel left out or unsocial, but if we keep holding on, others may understand one day. For example, a student in your class is being bullied, and you want to help him or her. However, others warn you that if you support that person, you will be treated the same way. Even though you disagree, you stay strong and help your classmate. Over time, your actions help that student improve, and eventually, others realize he or she is not so bad. As a result, the class becomes more peaceful.
Second, being good means following our faith and values, even when people around us disagree. Everyone has their own beliefs, and sometimes these beliefs come into conflict. We may have different values from our friends or even our partner. For example, some friends may think certain behaviors are acceptable, while you believe they are not because of your personal values. In these situations, it is important to stay true to what you believe is right. By protecting your values, you show self-respect and strength. Finally, people with courage follow their conscience, even when it is not the popular choice. Instead of giving in to peer pressure, they make decisions based on their values. For example, a student sees classmates cheating on a test and is encouraged to join them. Even though everyone else is doing it, the student refuses because they believe cheating is wrong. They choose to follow their conscience, even if it means getting a lower score or being seen as different.
In conclusion, being good means having the courage to do what is right, staying true to our faith and values, and following our conscience even when it is difficult. If we hold on to what we believe, one day we will feel worthy.
