Methodology

Study Design

The research design followed a structured four stage process. First, the team conducted an initial observation by watching the documentary Twelve Nights to understand issues within animal shelters. Second, information was gathered through consultations with professionals and field visits to shelters to ensure the survey instruments were grounded in reality. Third, the core research topic was clarified by using analytical tools such as the Fishbone diagram and the "5 Whys" method to identify a specific action point. Finally, the research questions were developed through online research and iterative discussions with an instructor.

Participants

The respondents in this study were the members of the general public, and a convenience sampling method was used for the questionnaire survey. The sample size was expected to be at least 50 people. To avoid the sample being too concentrated within the school, this study specifically set sample limits: a maximum of 2 student respondents from Da'an Junior High School, to ensure that the data have a certain degree of social representativeness.

Materials/Tools

The research tools mainly consisted of questionnaires and a list of items for collecting quantitative data. During the field survey, the research team used microphones and cameras to record the proceedings and prepared computer for presenting options to ensure respondents could clearly understand the questionnaire content.

Procedure

The implementation of this study was divided into two stages: team division of labor and on-site survey procedures. For team organization, members were assigned three roles to ensure survey efficiency: the speaker was responsible for proactively contacting the public, explaining the research objectives, and asking questions; the recorder was responsible for recording respondents' answers promptly and accurately; and the helper was responsible for showing laptops with options to help respondents visually understand the question and be easier to answer.

In the execution of the on-site survey, after arriving at the designated location, the research team first introduced themselves to potential respondents and explained the research project's objectives in detail to gain their willingness to participate. During the interviews, the team adopted standardized operating procedures, prioritizing inquiries about core research questions such as stray dog shelter and medical expenses. After collecting core data, basic background information such as age was asked. Before the interviews concluded, researchers would asked respondents if they're wished to know the final survey results after the research was completed, to maintain a good research interaction and feedback mechanism.

Special Considerations

To ensure data quality, procedural reliability, and professional ethics, strict operational guidelines were enforced during the administration of the survey. Researchers were required to select an appropriate location and communicate using a loud, clear voice. To maintain high consistency and decrease potential biases, the introduction script was kept the same for every participant, and team members were prohibited from switching their assigned roles during the process. Furthermore, maintaining professionalism and cultural decorum was paramount; researchers had to always remain respectful and were strictly banned from using inappropriate language. To project a professional image, the team was standing rather than sitting down, and they were prohibited from chasing after passersby. Finally, to avoid respondent got affected by others, researchers should interview only one individual at a time, ensuring each respondent could provide independent and objective answers.

Data Analysis

The analysis is divided into data processing and interpretive analysis. First, data is compiled, sorted, and visualized to identify emerging patterns. Second, the results are analyzed to describe region-specific findings and conclude the overall impact of financial perceptions on adoption behavior.
