Methods

Study Design

Our group worked together to learn more about the issue before creating the survey. First, we observed the topic by watching the documentary Twelve Nights, which helped us understand the situation of stray animals and animal shelters. After that, we gathered additional information by talking with professional people and visiting a shelter. These experiences helped us better understand the topic and design more accurate survey questions.

Next, we worked on finding a research topic. We used tools such as the fishbone diagram and the “5 Whys” strategy to identify important problems and possible causes. Through discussion, we selected an action point that our group wanted to focus on in the research.

After deciding on the topic, we developed our research question. We conducted online research, brainstormed possible questions, and combined our ideas together. Then, we discussed the questions with our instructor and revised them based on the instructor’s advice until we finalized the research question.

Participants

The participants in this study were people who completed our survey. Our goal was to collect responses from at least 50 people. To make the data more balanced, no more than 10 participants could be Daan students and no more than 5 participants could be Daan teachers. The participants included people from different backgrounds and age groups.

Our group used several materials and tools to complete the project. The main material was the survey, which was designed to collect data from participants. Posters showing the questions and answer choices were prepared to make it easier for participants to understand the survey. Procedure

First, students formed groups and assigned jobs for the survey process. The speaker approached people, introduced the project, and asked the survey questions. The recorder wrote down participants’ answers, while the helper held posters or materials that showed the answer choices.

Next, our group went to the survey location and conducted face-to-face surveys. We introduced ourselves and explained the purpose of the project before asking the questions. We asked the main survey question before asking participants about their age. At the end of the survey, we asked participants if they wanted to know the final results.

Special Considerations

Our group followed several important rules while conducting the survey. We made sure to speak loudly and clearly and selected a good place to conduct the survey. We avoided asking more than one person at the same time so participants would not feel confused or pressured. To make the survey fair and consistent, we used the same introduction for every participant and did not switch jobs during the survey. We also behaved respectfully by avoiding bad language, not sitting down during the survey, and not chasing people who did not want to participate. Finally, we tried to avoid bias so that the survey results would be more accurate and reliable. - Giving the survey - Speak loudly - Find a good place - don’t ask more than one person at the same time - consistent - don’t change the introduction - don’t switch job - be respectful - don’t say bad word - don’t sit down - don’t chase the people - avoid bias

Data Analysis

After collecting the survey data, our group organized the information so it would be easier to understand. First, we compiled all the answers together. Then, we sorted the data into categories and looked for patterns. Finally, we visualized the data using charts or graphs. After processing the data, we analyzed the results by describing important patterns and observations. We recognized that the results were region-specific and reflected the opinions of the people we surveyed.
