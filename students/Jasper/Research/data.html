2.Survey Question Results

Q1: 您近期是否去過可以接觸狗狗的場所？

The question was, "Have you recently visited a place where you can interact with dogs?" The purpose of this question was to find out how many people have recently been around dogs. This was a single-choice question and it collected categorical data. A pie chart was used to show the results because it makes it easy to compare the two answers. The most popular answer was "Yes," with 52% of people choosing it. The least popular answer was "No," with 48% of people choosing it. The results were very close, showing that about half of the people had recently visited a place where they could interact with dogs, while the other half had not.

Q2: 若您想親自接觸等待被領養的狗狗，您認為最大的困難會是什麼?

The question was, "If you want to meet dogs waiting for adoption, what do you think is the biggest difficulty?" The purpose of this question was to find out what problems might stop people from visiting and interacting with adoptable dogs. This was a single-choice question and it collected categorical data. A pie chart was used to show the results because it makes it easy to compare the different answers. The most popular answer was "I don't know," with 34% of people choosing it. The least popular answer was "No difficulty," with 4% of people choosing it. The results show that many people are unsure about the difficulties of meeting adoptable dogs. Fear of dogs and safety concerns were also common answers, suggesting that these issues may prevent some people from visiting adoption centers.

Q3: 根據以下選項，您認為哪種方式最能有效提升等待被領養狗狗的 關注度？

The question was, "Which way do you think can best increase attention for dogs waiting for adoption?" The purpose of this question was to find out which methods people think are most effective for helping adoptable dogs get more attention. This was a multiple-selection question because respondents could choose more than one answer, and it collected categorical data. A bar chart was used to show the results because it makes it easy to compare the popularity of different methods. The most popular answer was "Dog cafes and restaurants," which was chosen 62% of the time. The least popular answer was "Animal hospitals," which was chosen 8% of the time. The results suggest that people prefer places where they can directly see and interact with dogs. Dog cafes, restaurants, and school activities were more popular choices, showing that people believe direct contact can help increase interest in adoption.

Q4: 若您計畫前往動物收容所，對你而言最容易的交通方式是什麼？

The question was, "If you plan to visit an animal shelter, what is the easiest transportation method for you?" This question was asked to find out how people would prefer to travel to an animal shelter. It was a multiple-selection question because people could choose more than one answer. The data collected was categorical since the answers were different types of transportation. A bar chart was used to present the results because it clearly shows the differences between each option. The most popular answer was "Public transportation," which made up 64% of the responses. The least popular answer was "Walking," with only 12% of the responses. The results suggest that most people prefer transportation that is convenient and easy to use, such as public transportation and taxis.

Q5: 您認為促進狗狗與人類親近的最佳方式是什麼？

The question was, "What do you think is the best way to help dogs and humans become closer?" This question was asked to find out which method people think is most effective for improving the relationship between dogs and humans. It was a single-choice question because respondents could choose only one answer. The data collected was categorical since the answers were different types of methods. A pie chart was used to show the results because it makes it easy to compare the different choices. The most popular answer was "Online sharing," which was chosen by 44% of respondents. The least popular answer was "System improvement," with only 8% of respondents choosing it. The results suggest that many people believe sharing information online is a good way to help people learn more about dogs and build a stronger connection with them.
