Introduction

Abstract

Many animal shelters have too many dogs. To help increase adoption rates, we did a survey. We went outside and asked people if they have been to places with dogs, like dog cafes or farms. We also asked them about the best way to go to the shelter. Before the survey, we thought shelters had too many dogs because they lacked money. But we were wrong. Many people said they had no time and were scared of dogs. In fact, more people are scared of dogs than having no time. So we need to help people not be afraid of dogs. Through this survey, we learned what people think about dogs and how to help more animals get adopted.

Predictions

Before we did the survey, we made predictions about how people might answer each question. We wanted to compare our ideas with the real survey results later.

Q1: Before we did the survey, we thought that more people would choose No. We thought many people had not recently been to a place where they could meet dogs, such as a shelter, farm, zoo, or pet café. We predicted that about 75% of people would choose No.

Q2: Before we did the survey, we thought that the biggest difficulty would be not enough money. We thought this because having a dog can cost a lot of money, such as food, medical care, and other needs. We predicted that more than 50% of people would choose not enough money.

Q3: Before we did the survey, we thought that more people would choose dogs visiting public areas, such as parks or streets. We thought this would help because many people go to public areas, so more people could see the dogs and maybe become interested in adoption. We predicted that more than 20% of people would choose this answer.

Q4: Before we did the survey, we thought that more people would choose pick-up service. We thought this because shelters can be far away, and some people may not want to travel there by themselves. We predicted that about 50% of people would choose pick-up service.

Q5: Before we did the survey, we thought that most people would choose interaction and play. We thought this because spending time and playing with dogs can help people feel closer to them. We predicted that around 40% of people would choose interaction and play.
