Introduction

Our topic this semester is helping animals in animal shelters get adopted. The project we’ve been given
related to this topic is finding ways to increase animal adoption rates. Our group analyzed different areas
of the problem, and came to the conclusion that the smallest point where we can take action on is seeing if
animal adoption is an issue that can matter to policy makers. We will be having strangers of different ages
answer our survey questions. The goal of the survey is to see how much the general public feels about the
importance of animal adoption. What we hope to achieve with our survey is to get our results to show how
important animal adoption is to the public, which would make policy makers see the importance of the issue
and make policies to get animal adoption be taught in education.

Abstract

Animal adoption rates are low, we want them to be higher. In order to do this, we first need to find the
smallest point where we can take action. We cut the problem down into its component areas, picked the area
we
believed was the most effective, and then continually focused on smaller and smaller causes to find the root
cause: to see if animal adoption is an issue that can matter to policy makers, which is the smallest
actionable
point. With the smallest actionable point, we made a survey and administered it to the general public. Our
most
important finding was that a great majority of the general public believe putting animal adoption in the
education curriculum to be what policymakers should do to raise animal adoption rates. A majority of the
general
public also believe more face-to-face activities should be held for animals and animal adopters in order to
increase the rates. The results suggest that many people care about the issue of animal adoption, which
means
this issue is something worth mattering to policymakers.

Predictions

For question one, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that they know a little about animal adoption. We made this prediction because animal adoption isn’t taught in schools, so not a lot of people will know about it. Our prediction was very close to the results. For question two, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that they care a lot about animal adoption. We made this prediction because even if most people don’t know about animal adoption, humans still feel a natural empathy for animals, especially for pet animals like dogs and cats, and hope for them to have a home. Our prediction was very close to the results. For question three, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that policymakers should try to increase animal adoption rates. We made this prediction for the same reason above, that people want good for the animals and want them to get adopted. Our prediction was very close to the results. For question four, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would choose putting animal adoption as part of the education curriculum as what policymakers should do in order to increase animal adoption rates. We made this prediction because our group believes that the root cause for low adoption rates is that animal adoption isn’t taught to people, making it so not a lot of people know about animal adoption and think about adopting. However, that option ended up being the least picked, and the most popular being to hold more face-to-face activities for animals and animal adopters. For question five, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that animal adoption issues should be taught in school. We made this prediction because seeing how not a lot of people know about animal adoption, respondents might also think that it should be taught in school so that more people know about this issue for there to be more animal adopters.
