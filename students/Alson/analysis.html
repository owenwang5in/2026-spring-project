Analysis

Data Analysis

Our group asked 60 people to answer our survey question. Among them, the majority of respondents were aged 10-19, 52%. Only one person in each of the 60-69 and 70 and above age groups answered our survey. According to the data in question 1, the category with the highest frequency overall is 2 at 31.66% , which means more people only know a little about animal adoption. According to the data in question 2, the category with the highest frequency overall is 3 and 4 at 30% , which means most people think they care about animal adoption, but not a lot. According to the data in question 3, the category with the highest frequency overall is yes at 93.33%, which means most people think policy makers should do something to increase animal adoption rates. According to the data in question 4, the category with the highest frequency overall is holding more face-to-face activities for animals and animal adopters at 20.1%, which means more people think holding more face-to-face activities for animals and adopters can increase adoption rates. According to the data in question 5, the category with the highest frequency overall is yes at [ 93.33% ], which means most people think policy makers should put animal adoption issues to the curriculum to increase animal adoption rates.

Conclusion

In conclusion, the results were similar to what we predicted. Most people reported that they did not know much about animal adoption and shelter issues. However many respondents care about this, and they believe that face-to-face interactions between people and animals are more effective in increasing adoption rates than hiring more shelter workers. This shows that direct contact can help people develop a stronger connection with animals and encourage adoption. So we think policymakers and animal shelters should organize more face-to-face activities in the future to raise public awareness and increase the number of animal adoptions. Overall, the results suggest that many people care about animal welfare and are willing to support efforts to help animals find new homes.
