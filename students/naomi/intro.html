ʚ Introduction ɞ

✶Abstract

Animal adoption rates are low, we want them to be higher. In order to do this, we first need to find the smallest point where we can take action. We cut the problem down into its component areas, picked the area we believed was the most effective, and then continually focused on smaller and smaller causes to find the root cause: to see if animal adoption is an issue that can matter to policy makers, which is the smallest actionable point. With the smallest actionable point, we made a survey and administered it to the general public. Our most important finding was that a great majority of the general public believe putting animal adoption in the education curriculum to be what policymakers should do to raise animal adoption rates. A majority of the general public also believe more face-to-face activities should be held for animals and animal adopters in order to increase the rates. The results suggest that many people care about the issue of animal adoption, which means this issue is something worth mattering to policymakers.

✶Introduction

Many dogs stay in animal shelters without being adopted, and adoption rates remain low. We believe the main problem is that if the public thinks animal adoption is important, the policy makers will notice this issue. Our smallest actionable point is trying to see if animal adoption is an issue that can matter to policy makers, which can help improve adoption rates in the future. In this research, we want to know how the public cares about animal adoption issues, and experiences about dog adoption. To do this, we will ask people to survey to analyze their opinion.

✶Predictions

For question one, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that they know a little about animal adoption. We made this prediction because animal adoption isn’t taught in schools, so not a lot of people will know about it. Our prediction was very close to the results.

For question two, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that they care a lot about animal adoption. We made this prediction because even if most people don’t know about animal adoption, humans still feel a natural empathy for animals, especially for pet animals like dogs and cats, and hope for them to have a home. Our prediction was very close to the results.

For question three, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that policymakers should try to increase animal adoption rates. We made this prediction for the same reason above, that people want good for the animals and want them to get adopted. Our prediction was very close to the results.

For question four, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would choose putting animal adoption as part of the education curriculum as what policymakers should do in order to increase animal adoption rates. We made this prediction because our group believes that the root cause for low adoption rates is that animal adoption isn’t taught to people, making it so not a lot of people know about animal adoption and think about adopting. However, that option ended up being the least picked, and the most popular being to hold more face-to-face activities for animals and animal adopters.

For question five, we predicted that the majority of the respondents would say that animal adoption issues should be taught in school. We made this prediction because seeing how not a lot of people know about animal adoption, respondents might also think that it should be taught in school so that more people know about this issue for there to be more animal adopters.
