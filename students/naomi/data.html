Data

Our group asked 60 people to answer our survey question. Among them, the majority of respondents were aged 10-19, 52%. Only one person in each of the 60-69 and 70 and above age groups answered our survey.

We interviewed 60 people in total. Most of the respondents were aged 10–19, making up 52% of all participants. This was the largest age group in our survey. In comparison, only one person aged 60–69 and one person aged 70 or above took part in the survey. Therefore, the survey results mainly show the opinions of younger people on animal adoption.

In this question we’re asking about how much they know about animal adoption issues.It’s a rating scale question.

According to the data in question 1, the category with the highest frequency overall is 2 at 31.66% , which means more people only know a little about animal adoption.

In this question we want to know how people care about animal adoption issues.It’s a rating scale question.

According to the data in question 2, the category with the highest frequency overall is 3 and 4 at 30% , which means most people think they care about animal adoption, but not a lot.

In this question we want to know many people think policy makers should or shouldn’t increase animal adoption rates It’s a multiple choice(single selection) question.

According to the data in question 3, the category with the highest frequency overall is yes at 93.33%, which means most people think policy makers should do something to increase animal adoption rates.

In this question we're asking them what thing they think policy makers should do to increase animal adoption rates.It’s a multiple choice(multiple selection) question.

According to the data in question 4, the category with the highest frequency overall is holding more face-to-face activities for animals and animal adopters at 20.1%, which means more people think holding more face-to-face activities for animals and adopters can increase adoption rates.

In this question we want to see if people support animal adoption issues add in curriculums. It’s a multiple choice(single selection) question.
