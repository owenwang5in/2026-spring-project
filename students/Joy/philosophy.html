What is good

Good and evil are not just judged by people’s actions, but also the intentions behind those actions. A person may do something that looks good, but it’s done for selfish reasons, it may not be truly good. Therefore, understanding a person’s intention is important when deciding whether an action is truly good.
First, intention defines true goodness. Actions are not truly good if they are done only to receive praise or attention from others. For example, someone might help others to get rewards, or to go viral to let people see them and think they’re kind. But true goodness depends on intention rather than appearance. When people act with selfish motives, their good actions become less meaningful.
Second, sincere motives show real kindness. People apologise because they truly feel sorry, it demonstrates honesty and responsibility. Giving advice to help others improve can also show kindness, because the goal is to help the other person grow, not to judge them or want to be better than them. These actions reflect genuine care for others rather than a desire for personal gain. Sincere intentions make kind actions more meaningful.
Another important sign of true goodness is what people do when no one is watching. People with genuine kindness behave the same whether others see them or not. Doing good without expecting rewards or other’s attention shows strong character. If their purpose is to help people but not to be seen, then small acts of kindness done privately, such as helping someone quietly or supporting someone.
In conclusion, true goodness is not only about what people do, their intentions behind those actions are more important. Actions done for selfish reasons may look good, but it doesn’t represent true kindness. Genuine goodness comes from sincere intentions and the willingness to do good even when no one is watching.
