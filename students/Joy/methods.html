Study Design:
The project was carried out in four stages. First, the researchers watched Twelve Nights to gain a deeper understanding of issues related to animal shelters. They then gathered information through shelter visits and interviews with experts. After that, they applied tools such as the Fishbone Diagram and the “5 Whys” technique to analyze the root causes of the problem and identify a key area for improvement. Finally, with support from online resources and guidance from their teacher, they developed their research questions.

1. Initial observation of situation/subject
- Watch the documentary of Twelve Nights

2. Getting information so that you can design the survey accurately
- Getting information from professional people
- Going to the shelter

3. Find research topic
- Fish bone
- 5 whys
- Find action point

4. Come up with research question
- Do online research
- Putting all the questions together
- Discussing questions with instructor
- Revise the question with instructor advice

Participants:
The respondents in this study were members of the general public, selected through a convenience sampling method. The target sample size was at least 50 participants. To prevent the sample from being overly concentrated within the school community, limits were established on the number of respondents from Da’an Junior High School, with no more than two students included in the survey. This approach helped improve the diversity of the sample and enhance the social representativeness of the data.

- People that participated in the survey
- 50 people minimum
- 10 Daan students maximum
- 5 Daan teachers maximum

Materials/Tools:
The primary research instruments used in this study were questionnaires and data collection forms. During the field survey, the research team utilized microphones and cameras to document the data collection process. Computers were also provided to display the questionnaire options, making it easier for respondents to understand the questions and provide accurate responses.

- Survey (design for collecting data)
- Question list
- Microphone
- Camera
- Stationery
- Poster for questions and choices
- Laptop

Procedure:
The study was conducted in two stages: team preparation and field surveys. Team members took on different roles, including interviewing participants, recording responses, and displaying answer options on a laptop to assist respondents. Before each survey, researchers introduced themselves and explained the purpose of the study to obtain consent. Interviews followed a standard procedure, beginning with questions about stray dog shelters and medical expenses, followed by demographic questions such as age. Participants were also asked whether they would like to receive the final survey results.

3. Go to place for survey

4. Conducting the survey

- Ask the question before asking their age
- Ask if they want results

Special Considerstion:
To ensure reliability and consistency, the research team followed a standard survey procedure. Researchers used the same introduction script, remained in their assigned roles, and conducted interviews individually. They were also expected to communicate clearly and behave professionally, while respecting each participant’s choice to take part in the survey.

- Giving the survey
- Speak loudly
- Find a good place
- don’t ask more than one person at the same time
- consistent
- don’t change the introduction
- don’t switch job
- be respectful
- don’t say bad word
- don’t sit down
- don’t chase the people
- avoid bias
